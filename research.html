Statement

Research Vision

My research is driven by a fundamental question: how can we build AI systems that genuinely understand and assist humans? I work at the intersection of machine learning, computer vision, and robotics, developing algorithms and systems that can perceive complex environments, learn from interaction, and communicate meaningfully with people.

Specifically, I focus on multimodal deep learning for human-robot interaction — combining visual, auditory, and contextual signals to enable robots to respond appropriately in real-world, unstructured settings. I am also interested in making these systems efficient enough for edge deployment, where computational resources are limited.

I believe AI research should be grounded in real-world needs. My work aims to produce systems that are not only theoretically rigorous but practically deployable in healthcare, elder care, and assistive technology contexts.
